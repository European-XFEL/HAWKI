The following text is an excerpt from the Staff note on the policy for using generative artificial intelligence tools within the company. This guide aims to provide all staff members and guests with a brief guidance on how to responsibly manage this tool, supplementing the OpenAI terms of use without claiming to be exhaustive. It is the responsibility of the users to comply with these terms of use.

Prohibited and Restricted Uses

Generative AI Applications, provided by the Company, shall not be used for private purposes.

In particular, do not enter:

  • Personal data
  • Copyrighted data
  • Protected or confidential information
  • Safety/security protocols

Do not use Generative AI Applications for:

  • Automated evaluations of people (e.g., hiring, performance), this includes using Generative AI Applications for evaluating CVs.
  • Categorizing humans
  • Controlling personal safety equipment or executing hazardous tasks
  • Scientific work without critical inspection and proper disclosure
  • Plagiarism or unverified publishing of Output-Data
  • Producing or distributing unchecked or misleading Output-Data
  • Circumventing internal approval workflows or data policies

Risks and Mitigation - Good Practices

Privacy Violations

  • Never enter personal, confidential, or unpublished data
  • Use pseudonymization or obfuscation when evaluating content

Copyright, Plagiarism and Authorship. Be aware that:

  • Output-Data may be subject to copyright restrictions due to third party copyrights
  • AI cannot be credited as authors
  • Output-Data themselves are not protected by copyright of the AI. However, they may include elements derived from copyrighted works, which could pose a risk of copyright infringement (including plagiarism)

Hallucinating and Misinformation

  • Be aware that AI can "hallucinate" and generate incorrect or fabricated content
  • Always fact-check and edit Output-Data

Bias and Discrimination

  • Check Output-Data for discriminatory language or stereotypes
  • Use extra caution for any use cases with societal or reputational impact

Good Practice Checklist

  • Review: All AI-generated content must be checked before use
  • Disclosure: Clearly indicate if AI was used in content creation (via note, footnote, or citation)
  • Open Science: If research involves AI use, models, then data must be shared where legally and ethically possible
  • Oversight: All important decisions must involve human judgment
